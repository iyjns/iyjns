About

Netherlands' Capital

Amsterdam is an exciting place – it is the capital of the Netherlands and has over 80 thousand people. Amsterdam is a hub of culture, politics, and art. Of course, most people will hear the name “Amsterdam” and immediately think of coffee shops and canals – and of course, the famous Red Light District — but there are dozens of other interesting facts about Amsterdam – so let’s get started.

Amsterdam Facts

Amsterdam has the smallest house in Europe

The smallest house is in Amsterdam, and it is only 2.02 metres wide and 5 meters deep. These houses were built to evade property tax, as canal-front buildings were historically taxed based on their width.

There are 4 times more bridges in Amsterdam than in Venice

Amsterdam is already well-known for its canals, called Grachten – and not without cause. There are over 160 canals, which total around 100km. This means that Amsterdam has more canals than Venice.

View!
There are 2500 boathouses in Amsterdam

Unsurprisingly, the waterways of Amsterdam mean that a lot of people choose to live in boathouses. There are roughly 2500 houseboats in the city, many of which are over 100 years old. This means that Amsterdam has more canals than Venice.

View!
Amsterdam has the highest museum density in the world

There are over 90 museums in Amsterdam, giving it the highest museum density of any city in the world. There are some that are generally considered the best museums in Amsterdam, such as the Van Gogh Museum and The Rijksmuseum of art and artefacts.

View!
Amsterdam has more than 160 coffeeshops

These locations are strictly monitored and some are only accessible to locals, though most coffeeshops in Amsterdam are open to tourists as well – which is one reason why Amsterdam is such a popular tourist location.

View!
Heineken was founded in Amsterdam

Heineken is a popular beer all over the world now, but it was founded in Amsterdam in The Haystack brewery. Heineken lager was first brewed by Gerard Adriaan Heineken in 1873. In 2011 roughly 2.7 billion litres of Heineken lager were brewed. There is the Heineken Experience in Amsterdam.
